Happy to share that five of our papers have been accepted at the AfricaNLP 2025 workshop, co-located with ACL 2025! 

The accepted papers include:

1. **[HausaNLP: Overcoming Challenges and Making Progress in Hausa Language Processing](https://scholar.google.com/citations?view_op=view_citation&hl=en&user=Ne1nt4gAAAAJ&sortby=pubdate&citation_for_view=Ne1nt4gAAAAJ:ipzZ9siozwsC)**  
   **Contribution**: This paper presents our comprehensive work on developing NLP resources and tools for Hausa, one of Africa's most widely spoken languages.

2. **[AfroXLMR-Social: A Tailored Cross-Lingual Model for African Social Media Text](https://scholar.google.com/citations?view_op=view_citation&hl=en&user=Ne1nt4gAAAAJ&sortby=pubdate&citation_for_view=Ne1nt4gAAAAJ:zLWjf1WUPmwC)**  
   **Contribution**: This paper presents our specialized XLM-R model for social media content across diverse African languages.

3. **Automatic Speech Recognition for African Low-Resource Languages: Challenges and Future Directions**  
   [Available on arXiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2505.11690), 
   **Contribution**: This paper presents obstacles in developing ASR systems for African languages and proposes innovative solutions.

4. 1. **[Who Wrote This? Identifying Machine vs Human-Generated Text in Hausa](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2503.13101)**  
   **Contribution**: The first large-scale detector for Hausa machine-generated text, achieving **99.23% accuracy**
